Hi! My name is Md. Mazidul Haque Farabi, and I am a student, an author, and a web developer.

Apps that I built

The Humanitarian app took me almost 6 months to make and this is my best app yet. It is a dynamic social networking app that inspires acts of kindness. I made the app using Java with JSP on a tomcat server, and obviously javascript, html and css for the frontend, with MySQL for the database. The app has a secure login functionality that I would argue was the hardest task to complete. Also the app lets users post pictures and react and comment on other people’s posts. It’s now being hosted through Oracle cloud on a VPS.

I built Quotastic with the basic idea of helping people overcome depression, stress and sadness. The app lets people swipe through motivating quotes with a sooting background music and scenery to calm them down and get relief from anxiety. I built the app with Kodular.io and Github pages.

Cardwords is a vocabulary enhancing app that aids in certain exam preparations as well. The app features over 1600 unique and important words to learn from and also has a practice exam mode in order to let the users track their progress. I created Cardwords using Netlify and Github and the basic coding functionality was made using Javascript and also JSON for storing the words as objects.

Penalty Reflex is the first android game that I made. It’s a simple game that can also increase people’s reflexes which can help them in real life sports. The game lets players choose either a penalty taker or a goalkeeper role and subsequently either score or save goals. Points earned from that lets users try new staidums and buy different variations of balls or goalkeepers. I used Scratch along with Netlify and Kodular to make this one. It was fun making it too since I had to map animations of the ball and the keeper on the x and y-axis and added timer and fading buttons as well.

DoggosEat is a restaurant review app I particularly made after I realized that it would be helpful to a lot of young students like me who can’t afford to eat outside frequently and would love to know a comparative rating and analysis of different restaurants before going and buying the food there. Currently the app only has restaurants listed from Bangladesh, but I plan to expand it to most other countries as well in the future.

My thoughts and goals

I see myself as a creative individual and I belive that I have what it takes to change the world. And I already have plans on how to do that – which mostly include changing the perspective of people. I dream that one day all the people in this world would be defined by their kindness and generosity – the qualities that make us human, the qualities that are above all.

More on my plan

With that in mind, I created the humanitarian app and I am still working to make it better. I made it with a mission of inspiring kindness on a larger scale. Once someone helps anyone through any effort, he or she can write a post about it in the app. And everyone who sees the post and reacts to it, is actually influenced to do the same and so the person who did the act of kindness gets points. People do good when they see other people doing good and that’s a fact. For some, it may seem an egotistic way or a boast or show off, but to be honest, we all know it’s better to influence others through good than through their vanity in their bodily looks or luxury.

The app lets people connect with others they may know, and I made sure that all the information within the app is secure, and the login credentials are encrypted along with the passwords being hashed. Online courses from different sites and stackoverflow’s community answers helped me throughout the project to achieve that. I was also fully aware of not using any copyrighted materials such as images or icons in my app. Currently the app is available on Google Play Store (sadly not yet on App Store), and has over 1000 downloads.
